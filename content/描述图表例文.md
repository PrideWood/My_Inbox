### **Sample Essay 1: Bar Chart**

**Title:** The Number of Books Borrowed in Four Different Libraries  
The bar chart illustrates the number of books borrowed from four libraries—City Library, North Library, South Library, and East Library—over a six-month period.

From January to June, the City Library consistently had the highest number of books borrowed, peaking at 10,000 in June. In contrast, the North Library saw a steady decline in book borrowing, falling from 6,000 in January to 3,000 in June.

The South Library showed an upward trend, with the number increasing from 2,000 in January to 8,000 in June. Meanwhile, the East Library remained stable throughout the period, fluctuating only slightly around 4,000 books borrowed.

In summary, the City Library outperformed the others, while the South Library experienced the most significant growth during the six months.

### **Sample Essay 2: Pie Chart**

**Title:** Preferences for Learning Tools Among Students  
The pie chart illustrates students’ preferences for learning tools, categorized into textbooks, online resources, mobile apps, and group discussions.

Online resources accounted for the largest portion, making up 40% of students’ preferences. This was followed by textbooks, preferred by 30% of students. Mobile apps ranked third, with 20% of students choosing them as their favorite learning tool. Group discussions, on the other hand, represented only 10% of the total preferences.

Overall, digital tools such as online resources and mobile apps were the most popular among students, reflecting the growing trend of technology in education. Traditional methods, like textbooks and group discussions, were less favored but still used by a notable proportion of students.

### **Sample Essay 3: Line Chart**

**Title:** Global Smartphone Sales from 2010 to 2020  
The line chart demonstrates global smartphone sales from 2010 to 2020, measured in millions of units.

From 2010 to 2015, smartphone sales rose steadily, increasing from 300 million units to 800 million units. This growth became even more dramatic between 2015 and 2018, with sales peaking at 1.2 billion units in 2018. However, after 2018, sales began to decline, falling to 900 million units by 2020.

The overall trend shows a sharp increase in sales during the first half of the decade, followed by a slight decline in the later years. This suggests that the global market for smartphones may have reached saturation by the end of the period.
