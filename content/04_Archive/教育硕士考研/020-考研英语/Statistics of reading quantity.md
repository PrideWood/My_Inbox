The column chart clearly displays the statistics on the average number of books read per person in seven countries in 2013. According to the data, ==reading volumes== in the U.S., Israel, and Russia are the highest among the seven nations, with 56, 44, and 42 books per person, respectively. In contrast, Korea and China have the lowest figures, with only 11 and 4 books per person, respectively.

This trend is concerning, as ==extensive reading== is **as** essential to mental well-being **as** fresh air is to physical health. Through reading, we gain both ==companionship== and experience. First, a good book can be a faithful companion, bringing us joy when we are happy and offering solace when we are sad or lonely. Moreover, reading allows us to **experience worlds beyond our own**. Although few of us can travel far or live for over a century, we can **live countless lives through the pages of books**.

Even with the rise of TV, cell phones, and the Internet, which have reduced ==the prevalence of reading==, **nothing can replace the irreplaceable role** that extensive reading plays in enriching our lives.
